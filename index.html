PhD project
Making sense of Illustrated Handwritten Archives
The project aims to develop a technologically advanced and user-centered digital environment that provides access to archives containing handwritten notes and illustrations. This technological tool, that combines both image and textual recognition, allows, for the first time, an integrated study of underexplored scientific heritage collections and archives in general.

Background
In 2012, I received a Bachelor of Arts from the University of Utrecht, with a focus on language and linguistics, and in 2016 a Master of Science in Media Technology at the Leiden Institute of Advanced Computer Science (LIACS). There, I continued with a PhD in computer vision and Semantic Web technologies, applied to the field of natural history and biodiversity. Currently, I am working as a postdoctoral researcher at the VU in Amsterdam, where I joined the Knowledge Representation and Reasoning group. There, I focus on understandable, human-centric AI, within the MUHAI project.
